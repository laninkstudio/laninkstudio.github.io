The Meaning of “勇” (yǒng): The Strength to Move Forward

If you’re drawn to ideas like courage, bravery, determination, and inner strength, the Chinese character “勇” (pronounced “yǒng”) might be exactly the symbol you’re looking for.

It’s a short, bold character that embodies not just physical bravery, but the emotional and spiritual courage to face life’s challenges.

1. What “勇” Represents

The word 勇 means “courage” or “bravery”, but in Chinese culture, it carries a much deeper sense of moral strength — the courage to do what’s right, to stay true to yourself, and to keep going even in uncertainty.

It represents:

  • Bravery in action — the will to stand strong in difficult moments.
  • Courage of the heart — facing fear with honesty and compassion.
  • Inner strength — calm determination rather than aggression.

In essence, “勇” is the quiet fire inside — not loud or reckless, but steady, resolute, and purposeful.

2. The Origin of the Character

The character 勇 has ancient roots that tell a story of strength and willpower.

Its structure combines two elements:

  • 甬 (yǒng) on the top — an ancient pictograph representing a passage or something flowing forward.
  • 力 (lì) at the bottom — meaning strength or power.

Together, they form the idea of “strength that moves forward.”
In other words, true courage isn’t just having power — it’s the willingness to advance even when things are uncertain.

3. Cultural Symbolism

In traditional Chinese philosophy and literature, 勇 is one of the key human virtues.

  • In Confucianism, courage is tied to righteousness — “To see what is right and not do it is a lack of courage.”
  • In Daoism, courage is gentle — the courage to live simply, to let go, and to follow one’s natural path.
  • In modern Chinese culture, 勇 represents determination, perseverance, and strength of spirit — the courage to try, fall, and rise again.

Because of this, the word appears often in martial arts philosophy, calligraphy art, and motivational sayings.

4. Similar Ideas in English

While 勇 translates directly to “courage” or “bravery,” its spirit connects to several English ideas:

  • Courage
  • Inner strength
  • Resilience
  • Boldness
  • Perseverance

You could think of it as a blend of “courage” and “grit.”
It’s about quiet confidence — not showing off, but standing tall in your truth.

Just as the English word “brave” often carries a tone of nobility and sincerity, 勇 does too — it’s not just physical strength, but emotional fortitude.

5. Why People Choose 勇 as a Tattoo

  • Powerful meaning — Represents courage, strength, and resilience.
  • Bold and balanced form — The strokes of the character look solid, compact, and symmetrical.
  • Universal message — The idea of courage transcends cultures and languages.

It’s a popular choice for those who have overcome challenges, survived struggles, or simply want to carry a reminder of their inner strength and self-trust.

6. The Calligraphy Style - Lìshū (隶书)

This 勇 is written in Lìshū (隶书), often called Clerical Script in English, is one of the most elegant and historically meaningful calligraphy styles in China.

Lìshū (隶书), is famous for its balanced beauty — strong horizontal lines, gentle curves, and a flowing rhythm that makes each character look steady yet graceful. Its strokes have a unique shape often described as “silkworm head and wild goose tail” — rounded at the start, sweeping elegantly at the end.
